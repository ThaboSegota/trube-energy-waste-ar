How Energy & Waste Management Works

1

Energy Intake

The body absorbs energy from food through the digestive system, breaking down nutrients into glucose and other molecules.

2

Cellular Respiration

Cells use oxygen to convert glucose into ATP (energy), producing carbon dioxide and water as waste products.

3

Gas Exchange

The respiratory system exchanges CO2 waste for fresh oxygen in the alveoli of the lungs through diffusion.

4

Heat Radiation

Excess energy is released as heat through the skin via terrestrial radiation, maintaining body temperature homeostasis.

5

Waste Excretion

The kidneys filter blood to remove metabolic waste (urea, creatinine), while the lungs expel CO2 and the skin releases toxins through sweat.

Key Systems

🫁

Respiratory System

Lungs facilitate gas exchange - oxygen in, carbon dioxide out. Alveoli provide a massive surface area for efficient diffusion.

⚠️

Terrestrial Radiation

The body radiates heat energy as infrared radiation. This thermal regulation is essential for maintaining core body temperature.

💧

Excretory System

Kidneys filter waste products from blood. Urine carries dissolved metabolic waste including urea and excess minerals.

⚡

ATP Production

Mitochondria convert glucose + oxygen into ATP energy through cellular respiration, powering all body functions.
